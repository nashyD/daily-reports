◆ The economy

Labor Jun 5

May jobs report beats expectations; unemployment steady

US employers added 172,000 nonfarm jobs in May, well above the roughly 85,000 forecast, a third straight month of gains. The unemployment rate held at 4.3% and average hourly earnings rose 0.3% on the month.

Why it matters: the monthly labor report is the single most-watched gauge of US economic health and a primary input to Federal Reserve policy.
CNN ◆ May jobs
Prices May

Gas reaches a four-year high; inflation accelerates to 3.8%

The national average for regular gasoline rose to about $4.48 a gallon in May — roughly $4.56 over Memorial Day weekend, the highest in four years — driven largely by higher global oil prices. Consumer prices rose 3.8% year over year in April, the highest annual reading in nearly three years.

Why it matters: fuel costs and inflation hit household budgets nationwide and were the dominant economic story of the window.
CNBC ◆ April CPIAAA
Fed May 22

A new Federal Reserve chair takes office

Kevin Warsh was sworn in as the 17th chair of the Federal Reserve, succeeding Jerome Powell, whose term as chair expired May 15. The change came as the central bank held its benchmark interest rate unchanged in a 3.5%–3.75% target range.

Why it matters: the Fed chair is the most consequential single official for US interest rates, inflation, and the broader economy.

◆ Science, space & energy

Space May 22

SpaceX flies its largest Starship on a debut test flight

SpaceX launched the first flight of its upgraded third-generation Starship from its South Texas site, deploying 20 mock Starlink satellites on a suborbital flight. Despite an engine glitch and a hard booster splashdown, SpaceX and NASA characterized it as a successful step toward returning astronauts to the Moon.

Why it matters: Starship is the vehicle NASA's Artemis program is counting on for crewed lunar landings.
NPR ◆ Starship
Energy Through May

Big Tech locks in nuclear power for data centers

By May, every major US hyperscaler had signed at least one nuclear-power agreement to supply electricity for data centers, with announced projects totaling roughly 9.8+ gigawatts. A May deal paired data centers with co-located nuclear plants for up to 4 GW.

Why it matters: surging electricity demand from computing is reshaping US energy planning and reviving nuclear power.

◆ Safety, health & weather

Health Through May 28

US measles cases near 2,000, threatening elimination status

The CDC reported 1,983 confirmed measles cases across about 40 jurisdictions in 2026, on pace to exceed last year's three-decade high, with Utah emerging as a new outbreak center. Officials warned the country risks losing the measles-elimination status it achieved in 2000.

Why it matters: losing US measles-elimination status would be a notable national public-health setback.
CDC ◆ measles data
Infrastructure May 12

Criminal charges filed over the Baltimore Key Bridge collapse

The Justice Department announced criminal charges against two overseas ship-management companies and a vessel superintendent over the 2024 cargo-ship strike that collapsed the Francis Scott Key Bridge and killed six highway workers. Separately, Maryland reached a roughly $2.25 billion settlement related to the collapse.

Why it matters: the collapse was one of the deadliest US infrastructure disasters of the decade and disrupted a major East Coast port.
CNN ◆ Key Bridge
