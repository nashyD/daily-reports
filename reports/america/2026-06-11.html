◆ The economy

Data Jun 10, 2026

May CPI hits 4.2% year-over-year — the highest in three years — but the core stays contained

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported the Consumer Price Index rose 0.5% in May and 4.2% over the prior 12 months, the highest annual reading since April 2023. The detail that matters: energy did most of the work, climbing 3.9% on the month and accounting for more than 60% of the all-items increase. Strip out food and energy and core CPI rose only 0.2% on the month and 2.9% over the year — close to the Fed's comfort zone. Shelter rose 0.3% and food 0.2%. Both headline figures matched economists' expectations.

The split — hot headline, mild core — tells you the inflation is being imported through the oil market rather than broadening across the economy. That's a different problem than 2022's wage-and-services spiral, and the Fed treats it differently.

Why it matters: The price you feel at the pump and on your power bill is the inflation story right now; everyday goods and services are running far cooler. For anyone managing their own money, the read is that this spike is energy-driven and potentially temporary if the supply shock eases — but it keeps rate cuts on ice, so cash and short-term yields stay attractive and mortgage/borrowing costs stay elevated for now.
BLS · CNBC · Fox Business
Markets Jun 10, 2026

Energy is the swing factor: oil near $95 as the Strait of Hormuz stays disrupted

Crude has climbed toward $95 a barrel for Brent, extending gains as a U.S.–Iran conflict entered a second day of strikes and shipping through the Strait of Hormuz — a key global oil chokepoint — was disrupted. Iran's Revolutionary Guard said the strait would be closed to vessels; U.S. Central Command said commercial ships were still transiting. U.S. crude inventories fell 7.2 million barrels last week, a seventh straight weekly decline. The EIA's outlook assumes constrained Hormuz traffic keeps Brent averaging around $105 in June and July.

Why it matters: This is the mechanism behind the inflation print — a supply shock at a single chokepoint feeding through to fuel, then to the CPI, then to Fed expectations. The practical effect for households is higher gas and energy costs through the summer; the practical effect for markets is that energy prices, not the labor market, are now the variable to watch for what the Fed does next. Fed Jun 10, 2026

An in-line print keeps the Fed on hold; yields barely move

Because the CPI matched expectations rather than surprising higher, markets read it as a non-event for policy: futures put the odds near 98% that the Fed leaves its target range at 3.50–3.75% at next week's meeting. The 10-year Treasury yield was little changed around 4.52%, and stocks pared early losses after the data. First-quarter GDP grew at a 2.0% annual rate, supported by private investment and exports.

Why it matters: No rate relief is coming next week. For a borrower or saver, that means financing costs stay where they are and short-term savings keep paying — the Fed has signaled it will not cut into an energy-driven inflation bump until it sees the supply pressure ease.

◆ Markets & business

IPO Jun 12, 2026 (trading)

SpaceX's combined entity prices the largest IPO in history

SpaceX — which absorbed xAI (Grok and X) in an all-stock deal earlier in 2026 — is set to begin trading on Nasdaq June 12 under the ticker SPCX, at a fixed $135 offer price and a roughly $1.75T valuation, aiming to raise up to $75B. That would surpass Saudi Aramco's 2019 record as the largest IPO ever. The structure uses dual-class shares that concentrate about 85% of voting power with Elon Musk, so public shareholders will have little say on governance. In Q1, Starlink posted a $1.19B operating profit while the launch business and the AI unit ran losses.

Why it matters: A single listing of this size pulls a lot of capital and attention, and it gives public-market investors their first large, direct exposure to a frontier-AI business — bundled with rockets and satellite internet. For a personal investor the notable detail is the governance: a record raise paired with near-zero shareholder voting power is a tradeoff worth understanding before chasing the ticker.
