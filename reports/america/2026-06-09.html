◆ Economy & markets

Breaking Jun 5, 2026

May payrolls nearly double expectations at 172,000; unemployment holds at 4.3%

The Labor Department reported 172,000 jobs added in May, roughly double consensus, with the unemployment rate effectively steady at 4.3% (4.30% versus 4.33% in April). Leisure and hospitality led with about 70,000 jobs. The report describes a labor market that is reaccelerating rather than cooling. Markets read it as "good news is bad news": the benchmark 10-year Treasury yield rose to 4.54% on expectations that strong hiring keeps the Federal Reserve from cutting rates soon. New Fed Chair Kevin Warsh is scheduled to hold his first post-FOMC press conference on June 17.

Why it matters: A hotter jobs market plus higher long-term yields means borrowing costs — mortgages, auto loans, small-business credit lines — stay elevated for longer. For anyone managing their own money or financing a purchase, the near-term path of rate relief just got pushed back; the June 17 Fed meeting is the next signal.
CNBC · Schwab market update

◆ Jobs & investment in the Southeast

New Jun 2026

SMBC Group picks Charlotte for its second U.S. headquarters

Japanese banking group SMBC said it has chosen Charlotte for its second U.S. headquarters, citing roughly 2,000 jobs and a $50.5 million investment. It lands amid a strong regional picture: local leaders point to record-breaking growth, with the Charlotte Regional Business Alliance citing thousands of new jobs and over $1.4 billion in potential investment in the pipeline. Charlotte's unemployment rate sits near 3.6%, below the national rate.

Why it matters: A second major financial-services HQ deepens Charlotte's banking base and brings high-wage hiring, which ripples into housing, services, and local small businesses across the metro.
WCNC · WFAE Business
New Jun 8, 2026

Amazon–Corning fiber deal adds ~1,000 advanced-manufacturing jobs in North Carolina

Amazon and Corning announced a multibillion-dollar agreement for optical fiber and connectivity to supply Amazon's expanding U.S. data centers, creating about 1,000 new advanced-manufacturing jobs at Corning's North Carolina plants (average pay above $65,000) plus construction roles. The deal expands a fiber-optic technician training program with Catawba Valley Community College. Amazon says it has invested more than $20 billion and created 26,000+ jobs in North Carolina to date.

Why it matters: The national AI data-center buildout is translating into physical manufacturing jobs in North Carolina, with a community-college training pipeline attached — a concrete, in-state effect of an otherwise abstract tech trend.
About Amazon · Spectrum News

◆ Science & space

Update Jun 8, 2026

A strong (G3) geomagnetic storm reaches Earth; NASA preps an Artemis 3 crew reveal

A coronal mass ejection launched by an M1.8 solar flare on June 6 reached Earth around midday June 8, with NOAA and the UK Met Office issuing a G3 (strong) geomagnetic storm watch — enough to push auroras toward mid-northern latitudes and, at G3, create minor disruption risk for satellites and power grids. Separately, NASA set June 9 to name the astronauts for the Artemis 3 docking mission flying in Earth orbit next year; earlier in the week, ISS astronauts briefly sheltered in an attached SpaceX Dragon capsule (June 5) while cosmonauts worked a persistent leak on the station's Russian side.

Why it matters: Strong solar storms can degrade GPS, radio, and satellite operations and stress power grids, so they're worth tracking beyond the aurora photos. The Artemis 3 crew announcement marks the next concrete step toward a return to the Moon's vicinity.
