◆ Science & space

Breaking Jun 5, 2026

ISS crew briefly shelters in Crew Dragon as a Russian-segment air leak worsens

NASA ordered the five-person crew — two US astronauts, a French astronaut, and a Russian cosmonaut — to don suits and shelter in the SpaceX Crew Dragon on June 5 as a long-known leak in the Russian PrK transfer tunnel escalated from about a pound of air per day to roughly two. The crew exited the capsule and returned to normal operations in under two hours; NASA and Roscosmos say the structural risk remains manageable.

Why it matters: A safe-haven order aboard the ISS is rare and underscores the mounting maintenance burden on an aging station as planners weigh its end-of-life timeline.
Scientific American

◆ Sports

New Jun 6, 2026

Golden Knights take Cup Final lead on the fastest hat trick in Final history

Vegas beat Carolina 5-4 in double overtime in Game 3 on Saturday to lead the series 2-1. Mitch Marner recorded the fastest hat trick in Stanley Cup Final history in the second period to build a four-goal lead; Carolina answered with four third-period goals to force overtime before Vegas won. Game 4 is in Carolina on Tuesday, June 9.

Why it matters: A record-setting performance in a back-and-forth double-overtime game puts Vegas one win from a commanding 3-1 series lead.
NHL.com
New Jun 5, 2026

Knicks take a 2-0 NBA Finals lead with a one-point win in San Antonio

The New York Knicks beat the San Antonio Spurs 105-104 on the road June 5 to go up 2-0 in the Finals, extending their postseason win streak to 13 games. A late miscommunication between Victor Wembanyama and Stephon Castle sealed the Spurs' loss. Game 3 is set for June 8.

Why it matters: New York is two wins from its first title in decades, and 2-0 Finals leads are historically very difficult to overcome.
ESPN

◆ Weather

Breaking Jun 7, 2026

Severe-weather threat Sunday across the northern Plains and Ozarks

NOAA's Storm Prediction Center flagged June 7 for severe thunderstorms with large hail, damaging wind gusts, and an isolated tornado threat across parts of the northern Plains, plus an isolated tornado and severe-wind risk from eastern Oklahoma and southeastern Kansas across the Ozarks. Strong gusts are also possible in the mid-Mississippi Valley and Mid-Atlantic.

Why it matters: Active early-June storms put millions across the Plains and Ozarks under hail, wind, and tornado risk heading into the work week.
NOAA Storm Prediction Center

◆ Economy & culture

Deal Jun 1, 2026

Berkshire Hathaway agrees to buy homebuilder Taylor Morrison for $6.8B

Warren Buffett's Berkshire Hathaway agreed to acquire Taylor Morrison Home Corp for $6.8 billion, sending the homebuilder's shares up about 24%. It is one of several large early-June deals, alongside Fertitta Entertainment's roughly $17.6 billion agreement to acquire Caesars Entertainment.

Why it matters: A major Berkshire bet on housing is a closely watched read on where one of the country's most prominent investors sees long-term value.

Culture Jun 6, 2026

"Scary Movie" reboot sets a franchise opening record

The Wayans-led "Scary Movie" reboot opened to roughly $52.7 million — a franchise record — to top the June 5–7 box office, with "Masters of the Universe" debuting in second at about $30 million. The overall weekend totaled an estimated $184.7 million, among the strongest for this point in the year since the pandemic.

Why it matters: A strong R-rated comedy opening signals renewed theatrical demand for adult comedies, a genre studios had largely shelved.
