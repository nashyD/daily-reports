◆ Economy

Jobs Jun 5

May payrolls rose 172,000 — roughly double expectations

The Bureau of Labor Statistics reported nonfarm payrolls grew by 172,000 in May, about double consensus, with the unemployment rate unchanged at 4.3%. Gains were concentrated in leisure and hospitality, local government, and health care; financial-activities employment declined.

Why it matters: The labor market is the single biggest input to whether the Fed cuts, holds, or hikes. A hot number means borrowing costs — mortgages, business loans, credit lines — are more likely to stay elevated through the summer.

Markets Jun 5

Stocks had their worst day of the year on the strong jobs print

The S&P 500 fell 2.64% Friday — its worst session since October — and the Nasdaq dropped more than 4%, its worst day since April 2025, snapping a nine-week winning streak. Investors sold stocks, bonds, bitcoin and gold together as strong jobs data lifted rate-hike expectations; AI and semiconductor shares led the decline, with Micron, Marvell and AMD each down roughly 6–8%.

Why it matters: A broad sell-off across asset classes — equities, bonds, crypto and gold falling at once — signals investors repricing for higher-for-longer rates rather than fleeing to a single safe haven. It directly affects retirement accounts and the cost of capital for businesses.

The Fed Jun 5

Yields climb to 4.54%; FOMC meets June 16–17 under Chair Warsh

The 10-year Treasury yield jumped to 4.54% on the jobs data. The Federal Open Market Committee is scheduled to meet June 16–17, with a press conference at 2:00 p.m. on the 17th — the first under new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh. Per the CME FedWatch tool, markets now price at least one quarter-point hike by year-end.

Why it matters: The 10-year yield sets the floor for mortgage and long-term loan rates. The June meeting is the next official read on where the Fed thinks rates are headed, which shapes household and business budgets for the rest of the year.

Stanley Cup Final shifts to Las Vegas for Game 3

The 2026 Stanley Cup Final between the Vegas Golden Knights and the Carolina Hurricanes — which opened in Raleigh, North Carolina — plays Game 3 on Saturday, June 6 at 8 p.m. ET in Las Vegas, on ABC. In the NBA, the Finals between New York and San Antonio continued with Game 2 on June 5.
