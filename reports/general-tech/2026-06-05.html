◆ Your stack

Patch now May

Next.js coordinated security release — 13 advisories

Vercel shipped a coordinated Next.js security release patching 13 advisories spanning denial-of-service, middleware/proxy bypass, SSRF, cache poisoning, and XSS. This is a fleet-wide "confirm your version" event for anyone running Next.js in production.

So what for TSD: this is your whole client portfolio. Check every Next.js site you run (TSD app, Bisque, Sonderwerks, detailing-inventory) is on a patched version — middleware/proxy bypass and cache poisoning are serious on client sites.
Vercel ◆ security release
Release Jun 4

Next.js 16.2 — ~400% faster dev startup and agent-native tooling

The release reports a roughly 400% faster next dev startup, double-digit rendering speedups, and 200+ Turbopack fixes. It also leans into AI-assisted dev: create-next-app scaffolds an AGENTS.md, version-matched docs ship as local Markdown, and an experimental CLI lets agents inspect a running app from the terminal.

So what for TSD: a materially faster local loop on the framework you ship most, plus first-class hooks for the coding agents you already use to drive a running app. Worth upgrading a project to feel it.
Next.js blogInfoQ
Deal Apr 21

SpaceX takes a $60B option to buy Cursor

SpaceX secured the right to acquire AI-coding company Cursor (Anysphere) for a reported $60B later in 2026, or pay $10B for an ongoing compute partnership tied to xAI's infrastructure. Cursor is reportedly used across most of the Fortune 500.

So what for TSD: the dev tool you build in is now strategically owned-in-waiting. Not urgent, but a nudge to keep a second coding agent (Claude Code, Codex) in your workflow so a single owner doesn't dictate your toolchain.

◆ The IPO window blew open

IPO May 20

SpaceX files for the largest IPO in history

SpaceX filed to list on Nasdaq as "SPCX," reportedly raising on the order of $75B at a roughly $1.75 trillion valuation — which would be the largest IPO ever. The financials fold in xAI, acquired earlier in 2026, entangling Starlink, compute, and an AI bet in one ticker.

So what for TSD: nothing to do, but it sets the tone for the whole risk market. When the biggest names go public, capital and attention flow to the AI/compute layer your business rides on.
CNBC ◆ SPCX
IPO May 14

Cerebras pops 68% — the first blockbuster tech IPO of 2026

Wafer-scale AI chipmaker Cerebras priced its IPO and closed up about 68% on day one — the largest tech IPO since Uber in 2019 — before pulling back the next session. It reopened the tech IPO market and validated an AI-silicon architecture that isn't Nvidia's.

So what for TSD: a healthy IPO window signals an environment where AI-adjacent businesses get funded and acquired. Background context for how aggressively you lean into AI offerings this year.
TechCrunch ◆ Cerebras

◆ Hardware, space & robots

Hardware Late May

Nvidia enters the PC CPU market

At Computex, Nvidia unveiled its first PC processor — an Arm-based "RTX Spark" superchip co-developed with MediaTek, pairing a Grace-class CPU with a Blackwell-class GPU and the full CUDA stack. It's slated to ship this fall in Windows-on-Arm laptops from Dell, HP, ASUS, Lenovo, and MSI.

So what for TSD: mostly a "watch this" — a credible Nvidia laptop chip reshapes the PC market and, longer term, what local AI on a laptop can do for the kind of on-device tooling you might one day ship.
CNBC ◆ Nvidia N1X
Space May 22

SpaceX flies Starship V3 for the first time

The first Block 3 / V3 Starship completed its suborbital debut, deploying 20 mock Starlink satellites and surviving reentry, though the Super Heavy booster was lost on its landing burn. V3 is the configuration meant to become the operational heavy-lift workhorse — and it underpins the SPCX story.

So what for TSD: not actionable — included because it's the groundbreaking item of the window and it's the hardware behind the largest IPO ever.
