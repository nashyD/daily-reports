◆ Your stack

Funding Jun 5

Supabase reaches $10.5B valuation on a $500M round

Supabase hit a $10.5B valuation after a $500M funding round, with CNBC tying the surge to the "vibe-coding" trend — natural-language app building where the database/auth/storage backend has to be instant. Supabase is the default Postgres-plus-auth backend for that workflow.

No new framework security advisory crossed Next.js / Vercel / React / Tailwind in this window. One standing item to keep on the calendar: Supabase is deprecating legacy API keys by the end of 2026 in favor of publishable/secret keys.

So what for TSD: Good news for your stack's longevity — Supabase is now too well-capitalized to disappear, so building client apps on it is a safe bet. Action item: audit which TSD client projects still use legacy `anon`/`service_role` keys and plan the migration to publishable/secret keys before the end-of-2026 cutoff so nothing breaks in production.

Quantinuum raises $1.68B in Nasdaq debut

Honeywell's quantum-computing subsidiary Quantinuum raised $1.68B on the Nasdaq under ticker QNT at $60/share (per Barron's). It is the latest deep-tech name through the reopened IPO window.

So what for TSD: No direct line to client work — quantum is years from your tooling. The signal that matters is the open IPO window: capital is flowing to tech again, which keeps the vendors you depend on (Vercel, Supabase) well-funded and shipping.

◆ Hardware & infrastructure

Security Jun 3

Chinese-made circuit boards under Nvidia AI chips raise US security concerns

CNBC reported that the printed circuit boards beneath Nvidia's AI accelerators are largely Chinese-made, raising national-security questions about the AI hardware supply chain. The two US public PCB makers, TTM Technologies and Sanmina, have ridden the trade — TTM up nearly 500% in a year, Sanmina more than tripled.

So what for TSD: Nothing to patch — you rent compute, you don't own boards. The takeaway is macro: hardware supply-chain politics could push cloud-GPU prices around, which eventually flows into the per-token pricing your AI features ride on. Keep client-facing AI costs metered so a vendor price move doesn't blow up a fixed-bid project.

Infrastructure Jun 4

US data-center buildouts are slipping on power and permitting

The WSJ reported that major 2027 US data-center capacity projects are running behind schedule, bottlenecked by power-grid constraints, permitting, land, cooling and community approval. Foxconn and Intel separately announced an AI-server infrastructure partnership, underscoring that the constraint is now the whole stack, not just chips.

So what for TSD: Compute scarcity is real and structural. Practically, that means latency and capacity for hosted AI can get lumpy at peak — build retries, timeouts and graceful degradation into the AI receptionist and concierge so a busy provider hour never leaves a client's caller hanging.
