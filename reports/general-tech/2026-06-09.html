◆ On your stack — patch this

Breaking May 6–7, 2026 (still the required patch)

Next.js & React security release: get Turbopack apps onto 16.2.6 or 15.5.18

The Next.js and React teams disclosed a batch of vulnerabilities — one in React Server Components and eleven-plus in Next.js — spanning denial of service, middleware/proxy auth bypass, server-side request forgery, cache poisoning, and XSS. They affect the App Router across Next.js 13.x, 14.x, 15.x, and 16.x. The DoS (CVSS ~7.5) lets a crafted request to a Server Function endpoint spike CPU; the SSRF hits self-hosted Node servers via crafted WebSocket upgrade requests.

The trap: the first patched releases shipped an incomplete middleware fix. Turbopack-enabled deployments need 16.2.6 or 15.5.18 to be fully protected — not the earlier 16.2.5/15.5.16.

So what for TSD: Action item, today. For every client/demo site on Next.js — check the version. If you're on Turbopack (the default on Next 16), upgrade to next@16.2.6 and redeploy; on the 15 line, 15.5.18. Vercel-hosted projects still need the package bump and a redeploy — the platform WAF mitigations are a stopgap, not the fix. Self-hosted Node servers are the most exposed (SSRF), so prioritize those.

◆ Platforms & design

Feature Jun 8, 2026

Apple ships six OS dev betas — and gives users sliders to tune Liquid Glass

WWDC brought developer betas for iOS 27, iPadOS 27, macOS 27 ("Golden Gate"), watchOS 27, tvOS 27, and visionOS 27. iOS 27 leans on efficiency: smoother animations, faster startup. The design news is Liquid Glass — a year on from its 2025 debut, Apple refined it and, notably, handed users control with sliders that adjust its transparency and arrangement across devices. Apple also previewed homeOS for an upcoming "HomePad" hub (a HomePod with a 7-inch display and an A18 chip).

So what for TSD: Two things for your web work. (1) User-tunable translucency means you can't assume a fixed glass look — if you lean on glassmorphism in client UIs, test legibility against low- and high-transparency settings. (2) Liquid Glass keeps pulling the whole web's aesthetic toward frosted, layered surfaces; it's a cheap way to make an SMB site feel current, but keep contrast accessible.

◆ Hardware & the AI buildout

New Jun 8, 2026

Amazon and Corning ink a multibillion-dollar fiber deal — 1,000 advanced-manufacturing jobs in NC

Amazon signed a multibillion-dollar agreement with Corning to supply optical fiber, cable, and connectivity for Amazon's expanding U.S. data centers. It creates roughly 1,000 new advanced-manufacturing jobs at Corning's North Carolina facilities (average salaries above $65k) plus construction jobs, and expands a Fiber Optic Technician Training Program with Catawba Valley Community College. It rides Amazon's ~$10B NC cloud-infrastructure push; Amazon says it has invested $20B+ and created 26,000+ jobs in the state to date.

So what for TSD: The AI data-center boom is now physically landing in North Carolina, and it pulls a skilled-trades and contractor ecosystem with it — exactly the kind of established SMB that needs a real website and booking. Worth a note for Grant/Bishop: the supplier and trades base around these expansions is a warm outbound list as the build-out hires.
