◆ Model releases & capability

New Jun 2, 2026

Microsoft ships seven in-house "MAI" models — and a 5B coder is already inside Copilot

At Build 2026, Microsoft unveiled seven proprietary models trained without OpenAI data: MAI-Thinking-1 (its first flagship reasoning model, a sparse ~1T-param MoE with ~35B active and 256k context, claimed to match Claude Opus 4.6 on coding), plus MAI-Code-1-Flash, MAI-Image-2.5, MAI-Transcribe-1.5, and MAI-Voice-2.

The builder-relevant one is MAI-Code-1-Flash: a 5B-parameter coding model already rolling out inside GitHub Copilot across Free/Pro/Max in VS Code, claiming a SWE-Bench Pro lead over Claude Haiku 4.5 and up to 60% fewer tokens on equivalent tasks. This is Microsoft visibly building an OpenAI-independent stack.

So what for TSD: A 5B coder cheap enough to be Copilot's default keeps pushing the per-build cost curve down. It doesn't change your Claude-first workflow, but it's a reminder to keep an eye on cheap, fast coders for the high-volume, low-stakes scaffolding parts of client work.
simonwillison.net · Tech Times
Feature Jun 4, 2026

OpenAI rebuilds ChatGPT memory with "Dreaming" — and brings it to free users

OpenAI shipped a rebuilt background memory system that continuously synthesizes memory across conversations, auto-revises time-sensitive facts (e.g. "going to Singapore in July" becomes "went to Singapore in July 2026"), and produces a readable, user-editable summary. A ~5x compute cut lets memory reach Free/Go users; Plus/Pro get 2x more. US first, expanding over the coming weeks.

So what for TSD: The auto-revising "stale fact" pattern is a concrete design idea for TSD Concierge and Front Desk — any product that holds persistent customer context. Decaying or re-dating facts beats letting a knowledge base quietly rot.
openai.com · 9to5Mac

◆ Business & money

Breaking Jun 3, 2026

DeepSeek raising ~$7.4B in its first-ever outside round, at up to a $59B valuation

Bloomberg and others report DeepSeek is close to sealing roughly 50B yuan (~$7.4B) — one of China's largest private tech raises — at a $52–59B valuation. Founder Liang Wenfeng is reportedly putting in 20B yuan of his own money; Tencent (~10B) and battery giant CATL (~5B) are the largest external backers, with Beijing's state AI fund participating. It's the first time DeepSeek has taken outside capital.

So what for TSD: A well-funded DeepSeek keeps cheap, open-weight frontier models on the table. For most client work Claude stays the default, but it's worth tracking for any future self-hosted or cost-sensitive feature where you'd rather not pay per token.

◆ Policy & safety

Breaking Jun 4, 2026

Bipartisan "Great American AI Act" draft would freeze state AI laws for three years

Reps. Jay Obernolte (R-CA) and Lori Trahan (D-MA) released a 269-page bipartisan discussion draft proposing a federal AI governance framework. It would preempt state AI-development laws for three years — states keep authority over AI use — and require frontier developers above $500M revenue to publish safety frameworks, report incidents, and submit to semi-annual third-party audits. It would override Colorado's AI Act (effective June 30) and drew immediate opposition from labor and consumer groups.

So what for TSD: The rules above $500M revenue don't touch you. What matters is the direction: a single federal floor versus a 50-state patchwork directly shapes the compliance surface for SMB AI products you sell. Worth watching — it's a draft, not law.
Roll Call · FedScoop
Improve Jun 2, 2026

Anthropic expands Project Glasswing — Claude's vuln-finding reaches 150+ critical-infrastructure orgs

Anthropic extended Project Glasswing, its AI vulnerability-finding initiative, to about 150 new organizations across 15+ countries spanning power grids, water systems, healthcare, communications, and hardware. Its Claude Mythos model has already surfaced thousands of high-severity zero-days, including issues in every major OS and browser. Anthropic says it won't give Mythos general access until misuse safeguards for the offensive-cyber capability are finished.

So what for TSD: This is the front edge of automated AI security review. It's enterprise-only now, but the eventual shape — "run an agent over your stack, get a vuln report" — is something you could resell to SMB clients once the tooling commoditizes.
TechCrunch · Cybersecurity Dive
