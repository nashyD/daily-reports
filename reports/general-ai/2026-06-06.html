◆ Labs & capability

Capability Jun 5

Anthropic: 80%+ of May's merged code was written by Claude — and a call for coordinated restraint

Anthropic said more than 80% of the code merged into its own codebase in May was authored by Claude, and paired that disclosure with a warning: the field may need coordinated development restrictions if self-improving systems start improving faster than oversight can keep up (per Reuters).

The 80% figure is the concrete part. The "coordinate a slowdown" framing is a policy position, so treat it as a stance, not a commitment.

So what for TSD: A frontier lab is telling you, with a number, that agentic coding is now the default way real software ships. If you are still hand-writing boilerplate for client sites, you are leaving margin on the table — lean harder on Claude Code for scaffolding, migrations, and test coverage so a one-person studio ships like a small team.

Model Jun 5

Alibaba ships Qwen3.7-Plus: multimodal, 1M-token context, $2 per million tokens

Alibaba released Qwen3.7-Plus, a proprietary multimodal model with a 1-million-token context window, priced at $2 per 1M tokens — reportedly about 60% below its text-only Qwen3.7-Max (per VentureBeat via Techmeme).

Long context plus low price is the combination that keeps eating into per-token economics across the board.

So what for TSD: For a RAG concierge or document-ingest feature, a cheap 1M-token window means you can stuff a whole client knowledge base into context and skip some retrieval plumbing. Worth a price/quality bake-off against Claude for the cost-sensitive client tiers — keep Claude where reasoning quality is load-bearing.

◆ The AI trade

Markets Jun 5

The AI rally pulled back hard — Nasdaq's worst day since April 2025

The Nasdaq fell more than 4% Friday and the S&P 500 dropped 2.64% (its worst day since October), led by a sell-off in AI and semiconductor names after a hot jobs report raised the odds of Fed rate hikes (per CNN, TheStreet). Micron, Marvell and AMD each fell ~6–8%.

This is sentiment, not a capability story — the models did not get worse on Friday. But it is the first real crack in the "AI stocks only go up" narrative this year.

So what for TSD: If the AI-infrastructure trade is rerating, expect more SMB clients to ask "is this hype going to last?" Your answer is the boring one that wins: you sell working outcomes (booked appointments, answered calls), not GPU futures. A correction in chip stocks does nothing to a receptionist that books jobs.

◆ Applied AI & money

Funding Jun 5

Suno raises $400M at $5.4B; Generalist raises $400M at $2B for physical AI

Music-generation startup Suno raised $400M at a $5.4B valuation and passed 2M subscribers in February (per Bloomberg). Separately, robotics startup Generalist raised $400M at a $2B valuation to build "physical AI" for task completion (per Bloomberg).

So what for TSD: Suno is a live example of consumer-grade generative media at subscription scale — useful as a reference when a client asks for custom audio/jingles without a studio budget. Robotics is further from your wheelhouse, but the funding signal says "applied AI with a clear deliverable still raises," which is exactly the lane TSD sells in.
