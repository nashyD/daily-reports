◆ The frontier moves into reach

New Jun 9, 2026

Anthropic releases Claude Fable 5 — a locked-down version of Mythos, its most capable model

Anthropic made Claude Fable 5 generally available and rolled out the unrestricted Claude Mythos 5 to limited users through Project Glasswing. Fable 5 and Mythos 5 are the same underlying model; Fable is the version cleared for broad use by new safeguards that block responses in narrow high-risk areas (chiefly cybersecurity and biology) and hand those queries off to Claude Opus 4.8 instead. Anthropic says the safeguards trigger in under 5% of sessions on average. The company calls Fable 5 state-of-the-art on nearly all tested benchmarks — software engineering, knowledge work, vision, and scientific research — and the most capable model it has ever put into general release.

Pricing is $10 per million input tokens and $50 per million output tokens, roughly twice Opus 4.8. Through June 22 it is bundled into Pro, Max, Team, and seat-based Enterprise plans at no extra cost; on June 23 Anthropic pulls it from those plans and shifts it to usage credits, with a stated plan to restore it as a standard subscription feature later. The release landed days after Anthropic publicly warned that frontier models are getting dangerous enough to warrant restraint.

So what for TSD: Two concrete moves. (1) Test Fable 5 against your real client work — RAG concierge answers, the receptionist's harder calls, code on the TSD apps — this week, before June 22, while it is free in Pro/Max. If it materially beats Opus 4.8 on a task that matters, you have a free window to find out. (2) Watch the API price: at $50/M output it is expensive for high-volume client features, so keep routing logic model-agnostic and default to Opus/Sonnet for bulk traffic, reserving Fable for the hard cases. The safeguard hand-off to Opus 4.8 also means some client queries silently route to a different model — worth knowing if you ever benchmark consistency.

◆ The price war reaches consumers

Pricing Jun 9, 2026

Google cuts AI Plus to $4.99 and doubles storage — and Anthropic still has no budget tier

Google dropped its Google AI Plus plan from $7.99 to $4.99 a month and doubled bundled storage from 200GB to 400GB, with the change rolling out over several days from June 9. The tier includes video generation via Omni Flash, the Flow creative studio, and NotebookLM. The cut brings to U.S. consumers the kind of sub-$5 pricing Google and OpenAI already test in markets like India. Anthropic has not matched it — no localized pricing and no budget plan anywhere — which one investor quoted in the coverage framed as the start of a "commoditization era" where vertically integrated players like Google can grind competitors' margins.

So what for TSD: Consumer subscription prices falling does not change your bill much — you sell on the API and on outcomes, not on reselling a chat seat. But the signal matters for how you pitch: SMB clients increasingly have a $5 general AI assistant in their pocket, so TSD's value has to be the things that assistant can't do — wired into their booking system, their catalog, their phone line, their brand. Lean the pitch toward integration and custom data, away from "we'll give you AI."

Improve Jun 9, 2026

Gemini 3.5 Flash reaches general availability

Google moved Gemini 3.5 Flash to general availability, positioning it as frontier-level intelligence at roughly 4x the speed of comparable models. Flash tiers are the workhorses for latency-sensitive, high-volume tasks — exactly the profile of a website concierge or a routing layer that has to answer fast and cheap.

So what for TSD: A faster, cheaper frontier-ish Flash is a real option for the bulk, low-stakes traffic in a concierge or receptionist — the FAQ-level queries that don't need Opus or Fable. If a client is cost-sensitive, a tiered setup (Flash for easy, Claude for hard) can cut spend without users noticing. Keep your provider abstraction clean so you can A/B it.

◆ Money & momentum

Funding Jun 9, 2026

A heavy funding week: Ramp at $44B, plus robotics and satellite mega-rounds

The week's funding roundup was dense. Spend-management platform Ramp raised $750M led by Iconiq, GIC, and Ontario Teachers' at a $44B valuation. Industrial-robotics firm Standard Bots took $200M Series C (General Catalyst among the leads), and Finnish satellite company ICEYE announced a €450M Series F led by General Atlantic. Context for the whole market: Anthropic now sits at roughly $965B post-money after its $65B Series H closed in late May, ahead of OpenAI's last private mark.

So what for TSD: The money is still chasing AI infrastructure and applied automation, which keeps the tooling you build on cheap, fast-improving, and well-supported. The more direct read: well-funded vertical players (Ramp in finance ops, robotics in logistics) are the kind of companies whose customers — local SMBs — get pulled toward modernizing. The wave reaches Gastonia eventually; you're early.
