◆ The frontier got cheaper and more agentic

Model May 28

Claude Opus 4.8

Anthropic shipped Opus 4.8 with measurable gains on agentic coding and knowledge work, high-effort mode on by default for coding, and finer "effort control." Fast mode is reported at roughly 2.5× the speed and a fraction of the cost of the prior version, with base pricing unchanged.

So what for TSD: this is the model you build and ship with. Cheaper, faster "fast mode" directly improves the unit economics of the RAG concierge and any agent product you put in front of a client.
9to5Mac ◆ Opus 4.8
Model May 19

Google ships Gemini 3.5 Flash and Gemini Omni at I/O

Gemini 3.5 Flash is pitched as beating the prior 3.1 Pro on coding and agentic benchmarks at roughly 25% lower cost, with Google explicitly betting its "next wave" on agents over chatbots. Alongside it, Gemini Omni is an any-input-to-any-output multimodal model, starting with video, with a Flash variant for developers.

So what for TSD: a fast, cheap, agent-optimized frontier model is the tier you'd reach for on high-volume SMB automation. Worth pricing against Claude for the non-critical paths.
TechCrunch ◆ Gemini 3.5
Model May 5

GPT-5.5 Instant becomes the ChatGPT default

OpenAI made GPT-5.5 Instant the new ChatGPT default, reporting a sizable drop in hallucinated claims on high-stakes medicine, law, and finance prompts versus the prior Instant model. The same week it pushed ChatGPT into Excel and Google Sheets globally.

So what for TSD: the hallucination drop matters for any client-facing AI you deploy. The Sheets/Excel sidebar is also a marker of what an SMB can now do for free — useful to know when you scope a paid build around it.
OpenAI ◆ GPT-5.5 Instant

◆ The money and the coding-agent leap

Funding May 28

Anthropic raises ~$65B at a reported $965B valuation

Anthropic closed a Series H that nearly tripled its February mark and, per reporting, overtook OpenAI to become the most valuable private AI company, on run-rate revenue reported in the tens of billions. It's framed as likely the last private raise before an IPO.

So what for TSD: your core vendor is now one of the best-capitalized companies on earth and heading public. That's durability for the Claude ecosystem your whole delivery model is built on — a point you can make to a nervous client.
Anthropic ◆ Series HTechCrunch
Funding May 27

Cognition raises $1B+; Devin writes 89% of its own code

Cognition (maker of Devin, owner of Windsurf) raised over $1B at a reported ~$26B valuation. The number that travels: 89% of code committed at Cognition is now written by Devin, up from 13% in December 2025.

So what for TSD: that 13%→89% jump in six months is the single best data point on where solo-builder leverage is going. Plan your capacity assuming the agent does more of the typing each quarter.
Cognition ◆ Series DTechCrunch
Tooling May 18

Cursor's Composer 2.5 lands cheap and agent-first

Anysphere's Cursor shipped Composer 2.5, reporting strong coding-agent benchmark scores at aggressively low token pricing, built on an open Kimi K2.5 checkpoint. It lands competitively with the frontier coding models at a fraction of the cost.

So what for TSD: a cheap, fast coding model compounds your output. Keep a second agent tool in rotation so you're not single-vendor on the part of the job that's now most of the job.
Cursor ◆ Composer 2.5

◆ Voice, infrastructure & the rules

Product May 7

OpenAI ships reasoning-grade real-time voice models

OpenAI released three Realtime API voice models: GPT-Realtime-2 (its first voice model with GPT-5-class reasoning, parallel tool-calling, and a much larger context window), a translation model spanning 70+ input languages, and a low-latency streaming transcription model.

So what for TSD: you sell an AI phone receptionist. Reasoning plus parallel tool calls plus live translation is a direct upgrade path for that product — benchmark it against your current voice stack before your next receptionist pitch.
OpenAI ◆ voice models
Infra ~May 19

NVIDIA's Vera Rubin platform ramps to production

At Computex, NVIDIA said its next-gen Vera Rubin platform is ramping into full production with first cloud instances due in the second half of 2026, alongside a large new open MoE model. This is the compute substrate under every model you use.

So what for TSD: nothing to act on today, but cheaper inference downstream eventually lowers your API bill. A macro tailwind for anyone selling AI on per-call economics.
NVIDIA ◆ Computex 2026
