◆ The money is moving to physical AI

Funding June 11

Prometheus raises $12B at a $41B valuation to build an "artificial general engineer"

Prometheus — the physical-AI startup co-founded by Jeff Bezos and former Verily co-founder Vik Bajaj — announced a $12B raise at a $41B valuation on June 11, backed by Bezos himself plus JPMorgan Chase, Goldman Sachs and BlackRock. It's the company's second round; it launched late last year with $6.2B.

The pitch is software that automates the design and manufacturing of complex physical systems — "from jet engines to drug compounds." This is the same thesis driving the broader 2026 funding wave: not another chatbot, but AI that does engineering work in the real world.

So what for TSD: Nothing to build against here directly — but it tells you where the smart money thinks the moat is. Pure text-generation is commoditizing fast; durable value is sitting closer to a specific trade or workflow. TSD's edge is the same logic one tier down: an AI bolted to a plumber's intake or an importer's catalog beats a generic assistant.

◆ Open weights are closing on the frontier

Model June

MiniMax M3 is now the strongest open-weight coding model — and you can self-host it

MiniMax M3 took the top of the open-weight SWE-Bench Pro leaderboard at 59.0%, edging Kimi K2.6's 58.6%, and it pairs that with a 1-million-token context window and native multimodal computer-use. The headline isn't the benchmark — it's the license: this is open-weight, so you can run it on your own (or rented) hardware instead of metering every token through a frontier API.

So what for TSD: This is the lever for margin on managed-AI work. A near-frontier open-weight coder/agent means TSD Concierge or back-office automations can run on a flat monthly compute bill instead of per-token API costs that scale with the client's traffic. Worth a test: stand one up behind a single client demo and compare the unit economics against the Claude/Gemini API line.

◆ What's still in the wings

Anticipation expected June

Gemini 3.5 Pro and Claude Sonnet 4.8 are both expected before month-end

Google's Gemini 3.5 Pro — a 2M-token context with a "Deep Think" reasoning mode — was announced at I/O on May 19 but is still in limited Vertex preview, with GA targeted for June. Sundar Pichai's own framing was "give us until next month." Claude Sonnet 4.8 is likewise expected before the month closes, after Fable 5 shipped last week. Treat "June" as a target, not a guarantee — launches slip.

So what for TSD: Don't hard-wire client AI features to a specific model this month. Keep the model behind a swappable config so when 3.5 Pro or Sonnet 4.8 lands — usually cheaper-per-capability than what it replaces — you can flip clients over without a rebuild. That swap-on-command capability is itself a Managed-AI retainer selling point.
